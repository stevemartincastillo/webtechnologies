Brief History:

Welcome to the Central Region of Belize—a place where history and adventure come together.
The Central Region is home to the capital city, Belmopan, and the largest city, Belize. Back in the late 1710s, brave British baymen set up their homes along our coasts. Due to its lush and rich jungle, logwood and mahogany were extracted and exported to Europe, providing economic value to its settlers. One of the most important chapters in our history is the battle of St. George’s Caye, which occurred in September 1798. In this battle, the Baymen, along with the slaves, defeated the Spaniards in a naval battle won by navigating the shallow channels around the cayes. This region is mainly populated with Creoles, who are the result of Baymen and African slaves. Linkage to the English-speaking Caribbean islands is very strong, and this linkage influences the music of this region.
