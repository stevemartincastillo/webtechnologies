Brief History:

Back in 1883, the brave Ketchi and Mopan Mayas left Guatemala to escape persecution, seeking a new beginning and a safe haven. Their journey was marked by hardship and determination as they navigated rugged terrains and unfamiliar landscapes. After arriving in Belize, these courageous families established the vibrant village of San Antonio in Toledo. Here, they built a close-knit community that held fast to their traditions while adapting to new surroundings. Over time, San Antonio became a beacon of Maya heritage, preserving ancient customs, language, and rituals that have been passed down through generations. Today, the legacy of the Ketch and Mopan Mayas is celebrated through colorful festivals, communal gatherings, and the everyday lives of the villagers. Their story is a powerful reminder of resilience, unity, and the enduring strength of cultural identity that continues to inspire us all.
