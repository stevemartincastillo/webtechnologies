Brief History:

Let me take you on a quick journey through the story of Gales Point Manatee which is one of the villages in Belize Rural — also known as Manatee village.
Nestled in the serene Southern Lagoon, Gales Point Manatee began as an early maroon community. This means it was founded by brave souls—ex-enslaved people who chose freedom over oppression by escaping the control of colonial slave masters. They sought a place to live independently and built self-sufficient communities in the hinterlands. Around the late 1700s and early 1800s, ex-enslaved folks from nearby areas like the Sibun River, Runaway Creek, Mullins River, and Main River came together here to create a new beginning. Their legacy of resilience and hope still echoes in our region today. Come along and feel the spirit of freedom in every ripple of the Southern Lagoon!
