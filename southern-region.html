Brief History:

For our Southern Region we share in the incredible journey of the Garifuna People—a vibrant blend of African slaves and Carib Indians, who were exiled from St. Vincent by the British in 1796. Due to their aggressive militant tendencies they were transported to the islands of Honduras, and later, during the republic revolution in the 1830s, they migrated to escape persecution and landed in Beautiful Belize. Thanks to their isolation, many of their unique traditions and cultural practices have been beautifully preserved to this day, enriching our region with their enduring spirit and heritage. Each year, there is a celebration of the Garifuna’s arrival to Belize around 19th November. This day highlights many of the cultural aspects of the people groups.
